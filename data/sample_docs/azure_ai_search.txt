Azure AI Search is a managed search service that supports keyword, vector, and hybrid retrieval. For semantic retrieval, documents are converted into dense embedding vectors, usually with an Azure OpenAI embedding model, and stored in a vector field. At query time the question is embedded the same way and the service finds the nearest vectors by meaning rather than exact words. Approximate nearest-neighbor search uses the HNSW algorithm, short for hierarchical navigable small world, which stays fast even over millions of vectors. Hybrid search combines vector similarity with traditional BM25 keyword scoring, and an optional semantic ranker can rerank the top results. This is the retrieval backbone of most retrieval-augmented generation systems on Azure: documents are chunked, embedded, and indexed, and then the most relevant chunks are passed to a language model as grounding context so its answers are based on your data.
